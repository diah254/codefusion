How I Founded Codefusion in 2024

7 min read Diah M
Back to Blog

Starting a company is rarely the heroic leap people imagine it to be. For me, Codefusion began with a simple observation: too many businesses around me had great ideas but no reliable way to bring them to life digitally. I knew I could help — and in 2024, I decided to stop waiting for the right time and just start.

The idea

Codefusion was born from my passion for building things that work beautifully. I wanted to create a studio that combined solid engineering with thoughtful design — a place where clients weren't just getting code, but getting real solutions. The name itself reflects the mission: fusing different technologies, skills, and ideas into something cohesive and powerful.

The hardest part: starting

The first few weeks were the hardest. No clients, no team, just me, a laptop, and a lot of self-doubt. I had to learn things no tutorial teaches — how to price my work, how to talk to clients, how to manage my time without a boss setting deadlines. These were lessons I could only learn by doing.

First projects

My first clients came through word of mouth — friends who needed websites, small businesses that needed apps. I under-charged at first (classic mistake), but those early projects built my portfolio, sharpened my skills, and taught me what clients actually need vs. what they say they need. Every project since has been a lesson.

Where Codefusion is today

Today Codefusion is growing steadily. I work on web development, mobile apps, UI/UX design, and cyber security projects. The journey has been challenging and incredibly rewarding. If you're thinking of starting something, my advice is this: start small, stay consistent, and never stop learning.

The best time to start was yesterday. The second best time is right now.
