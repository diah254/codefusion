How I Founded Codefusion in 2024

February 10, 2026 6 min read Diah

Two years ago, I was a solo developer taking small freelance gigs. Today, Codefusion has delivered 50+ projects. Here's how it happened — the mistakes, the wins, and what I'd do differently.

The Beginning

2024 started like any other year. I had a laptop, internet, and a reputation for delivering clean code on time. A client asked if I could "just add a small feature." That small feature turned into a full SaaS platform. That SaaS platform turned into a retainer. And that retainer made me realize: I was already running an agency, I just hadn't named it yet.

Naming the Dream

Codefusion wasn't my first name choice. I went through DevStudio, ByteCraft, and NxGen before landing on something that felt right. "Code" for what we build. "Fusion" for how we blend design, engineering, and strategy into one cohesive product.

First Hire, First Lesson

Hiring my first developer was terrifying. I had never managed anyone. The first month was rough — mismatched expectations, different coding styles, late deliveries. But we learned to document processes, use Git properly, and communicate daily. That developer is now my lead engineer.

"Your first team member teaches you more about leadership than any book ever will."

The Pivot Moment

Mid-2024, we almost shut down. Three major clients delayed payments simultaneously. Cash flow dried up. I learned the hard way that contracts matter, deposits protect you, and diversification isn't optional. We survived by focusing on smaller, faster projects while rebuilding our pipeline.

Where We Are Now

  • 50+ projects delivered across 4 countries
  • Team of 5 developers, 2 designers
  • Office in Nairobi's Kilimani district
  • Clients from startups to enterprises
  • Still coding every single day

Advice for Aspiring Founders

Start before you're ready. Build in public. Charge what you're worth. And never, ever compromise on code quality — it's the only thing clients remember when everything else goes wrong.
